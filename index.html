The problem

AI can copy what your experts know. It can't copy how they decide.

Every AI model available today has already absorbed the general knowledge of your industry. That's not your edge anymore. It's the baseline everyone starts from.

What's left is judgment. It's knowing whether a spike is auction pressure or ad fatigue. It's the exceptions to the rules. It's the moments your best people do something no model would, and turn out to be right.

That judgment doesn't show up in a playbook. It lives in your team's heads, and nothing you rely on today can adequately capture it, let alone put it to work if they leave.

How judgment is lost

Judgment leaves with people

Retirement, turnover, promotions. Years of calibrated decision-making can walk out the door with a two-week notice, leaving teams to relearn what was already known.

Reasoning isn't captured

SOPs, wikis, and screen recordings document what happened. They don't capture why an expert made that decision—the context, tradeoffs, and intuition behind it.

Experts can't explain instinct

Psychologists call it the expert blind spot: experts can't articulate most of their own decision logic. The most valuable judgment only emerges while they're actually doing the work.

Every new AI model makes your team's knowledge more common. Your new edge is the judgment you haven't yet captured and put to work. Arciom captures decisions as they happen: every month you wait to start capturing your team's judgment isn't just delay, it's revenue left on the table you'll never get back.

How it works

One loop: capture, prove, grow.

No decision is stored just because it happened. It's stored because it's been tested and it held up.

  1. Capture

    Arciom records your performance marketer working: screen and voice, as they think out loud. A few short follow-up questions after each session capture the reasoning behind the calls.

  2. Prove

    Every decision is tested against a frontier AI model of your choice (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and more). Only the decisions where your expert genuinely outperforms it get kept.

  3. Grow (digital twin formation)

    With enough proven decisions and the permissions you grant, that judgment grows into a digital twin: an asset tied to your expert that's capable of real work, not a static snapshot of it.

And it keeps getting smarter. Because Arciom tracks the result of every change, the digital twin sees the cause and effect of each decision it's involved in. Judgments that keep winning gain weight; ones that stop working get flagged and re-examined. The digital twin learns from the market, not just the expert.

Under the hood

The trust ladder

The digital twin earns more responsibility as it proves itself — never before.

You don't hand over your Google Ads account. That's not how Arciom works. The digital twin operates through permissions you grant, moving up a ladder where every step is earned by evidence, not assumption.

Rung 01

Incubate

The digital twin's brain is forming: it watches the work, connects decisions to outcomes, and asks your expert clarifying questions. It doesn't advise anyone yet.

Rung 02

Recommend

The digital twin begins suggesting changes, grounded in its proven judgment. Your team reviews each recommendation and decides what actually happens in the account.

Rung 03

Assist

The digital twin makes the change itself, but nothing goes live until a human approves it. Every action still passes through your team first.

Rung 04

Run

Once proven, the digital twin handles routine work around the clock, freeing your team to focus on strategy and the accounts that need their full attention.

Your marketer isn't replaced by their digital twin, they just spend less time on repetitive execution and more on the judgment calls only they can make. Stop the digital twin at any rung; nothing advances without a track record behind it.

And you don't wait for the ladder to pay off: weekly governance summaries begin during incubation, before the digital twin's first recommendation.

Governance summaries

Leadership shouldn't have to wait a month to learn what the spend did.

Most executives get a paid media update once a month, after the money is gone. Arciom sends a weekly governance summary straight to leadership: which decisions were made, which ones moved ROI, and which didn't.

It's not another dashboard to log into. It's a short, plain-language report that lets a head of marketing or CEO see the connection between decisions and outcomes, whether the work is done by an internal team, an outside agency, or a digital twin.

Decision-level reporting is possible because of the attribution built into the loop: every strategic change is detected as it happens, and results are tracked back to the decision that caused it.
